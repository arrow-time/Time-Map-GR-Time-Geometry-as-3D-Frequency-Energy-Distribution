Consequently, the spatial distribution of resonant frequencies across all sites is the time curvature of GR.

Continuum Limit: GR is formulated as a continuous field. This discrete resonant system approximates that continuous field in the appropriate limit.

In this way, the abstract geometric time curvature of GR is concretely represented as a frequency distribution across spatial sites.

The Nature of Electromagnetic Waves and Energy: Within this physical model, an electromagnetic wave is essentially a transverse wave propagating through the spatial site network — sites that possess spin, resonant, and other degrees of freedom. The frequency of the electromagnetic wave is exactly the frequency of the spatial sites. And since the frequency of an electromagnetic wave is directly related to its energy (E=hν), the frequency of a spatial site also directly represents energy.
